Navigating the intricate plotlines of 15 seasons can be a challenge for viewers trying to keep up with the most important aspects of "Supernatural," a series on The CW. However, it becomes more manageable when fans are aware of the plot elements that tie the story together. Sam and Dean Winchester have faced angels, demons, ghosts, monsters, and even God, but a few key plot threads are essential. Demon Azazel could only attack John and Mary in 1983 due to a deal they made. Mary made the pact after Azazel killed John in 1973, agreeing that he could enter their home ten years later. Jack's storyline spans the final three seasons of "Supernatural," revealing he's a Nephilim, the offspring of an angel and a human. As children of angels, Nephilim are more powerful than their parents, and Jack, being the son of the archangel Lucifer, is the strongest. Initially, the series downplayed Jack's Nephilim status, but it later became crucial as he could absorb God's powers and ultimately defeated Chuck, taking his place as the ruler of the universe.
